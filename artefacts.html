Units 1-3 Collaborative discussion 1

Initial Post

Background Information (ACM Ethics, N.D.)

Corazón is a company that sells implantable heart health monitoring device coming with a smart phone app for wireless control. The implant should only be accessible through short-range wireless connections which could only happen when the phone and the implant are in a close distance. Recently, a vulnerability in the wireless connectivity has been found by a researcher. In the researcher’s proof-of-concept demonstration, if a second device is in a close enough distance, it could modify commands being sent to the implant to force a device reset

Action from company

In response to this founding of vulnerability, the company’s technical leaders had been working with the researcher, and the researcher later concludes that the risk of harm with this attack is negligible, considering the device’s limited capabilities (ACM Ethics, N.D.).

Analysis of Code of ethics

The article provided by the Association of Computing Machinery has stated how the company complies with its code of ethics. For example, Corazón’s work with charities to help reduced access patients below the poverty line corresponds to Principle 1.1 – Contribute to society and to human well-being, acknowledging that all people are stakeholders in computing (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018). This charity actions may enable less-privileged people to gain access to benefits of technology.

Besides, Corazón had an open bug bounty program which helped to spot potential vulnerabilities corresponds to Principle 2.9 – Design and implement systems that are robustly and usably secure (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018). Use of cryptographic standard algorithms also corresponds to Principle 2.6 – Perform work only in areas of competence (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018). On the contrary, if the company tries to hide the known issues from government officials, it may result in jurisdictional consequences. For instance, FDA requires manufacturers to mandatorily report if the malfunction will likely to cause serious injuries or death (U.S. Food & Drug Administration, 2022).

When comparing against the British Computer Society’s Code of Conduct, Corazón is complying with this code as well. For the first principle – You make IT for everyone, Corazón’s charity act and willingness to work with government officials have indicated that the company has due regard for public health, and promotes equal access to the benefits of IT (The British Computing Society, 2022). Furthermore, the open bug bounty program corresponds to the second principle about professional competence and integrity. The company has shown respect of alternative viewpoints and accepted criticisms of work (The British Computing Society, 2022).

All in all, Corazón has set up a good example of upholding IT professionalism and ethics. Limitation is that no information regarding the discussion between the researcher and the company’s technical department can be sourced at the time being. There is uncertainty if the conclusion of negligible risk was drawn from a unbiased way.

Unit 1 Reflective Activity 1

According to Stahel et al (2016)’s findings, the consents issue in e-Health technology was among the top 20 items identified in the literature. As e-Health systems may collect and store users’ personal information, how the systems store and use the information may involve users’ consent and invoke ethical issues.

Jokinen and Suhonen (2021) pointed out that information sharing is the most concerning ethical issue in the use of e-Health services, despite the fact that information sharing has helped to enhance quality healthcare for citizens. From the results of the review, many citizens would like to be able to choose whether to give informed consent for sharing of personal data. In turn, the practice of informed consent would improve the trust level of e-Health services. Besides, the authors noted that human privacy and integrity should be respected. Citizens should have the right to refuse the use of e-Health services.

According to the GDPR, the processing of personal data is generally prohibited unless with the data subject’s consent. If the organization fails to implement the consent appropriately, it may be subject to substantial fines under the UK GDPR Article 83(5)(a) (Information Commissioner’s Office, N.D.). In Hong Kong, Electronic Health Record Sharing System Ordinance is established to govern the collection and sharing of personal information through e-health services. If the regulation is breached, such as unauthorized access to e-health system data, or use of such information for direct marketing, the individual can be subject to penalty or imprisonment (Electronic Health Record Sharing System, 2021).

 

It can be seen that consent management plays an important role in e-Health services. Recently, researchers have been working on solutions in this realm. For instance, Genestier et al (2017) have suggested the use of blockchain technology to manage consent to data access. The newly proposed solution can enable users to fine-grain their consent and the consent can be shared across different applications. Fan et al (2011) have also proposed the Data Capture and Auto Identification Reference (DACAR) project to address the issues of handling sensitive data, security, and large-scale deployments.

 

As a developer, we should have due regard for the third parties’ legitimate rights and always uphold professional responsibility (BCS, 2022). In case there are doubts about breaching related regulations on personal data processing, we should actively seek help and voice our concerns.

Unit 6 Wiki Activity

Review of Asthma Control Questionnaire

I have chosen the Asthma Control Questionnaire (ACQ) developed and validated by Juniper et al (1999). The authors stated that the questionnaire can evaluate and discriminate strongly among the cases and can be used to measure asthma control confidently.

The questionnaire consists of 7 questions. 6 questions are to be filled in by the patient and 1 question is to be filled in by clinical staff. Of the 6 questions to be filled in by the patient, 5 of them are asking the patient to rate the severity or frequency of the symptoms. The questionnaire provides 6 options for the patient to choose from. The options are described in words instead of numerical values. For example, when asking for the frequency of being woken up by asthma during the night, the options include never, hardly ever, a few times, several times, many times, a great many times, and unable to sleep. I understand the questions aim to find out how severely the patient is impacted by asthma. Kronsnick pointed out the audience has to have a clear understanding of each point of the scale. As wordings like “many times” may vary according to person, introducing numeric values to the scale will give a more objective and quantitative response.

For the question to be filled in by the clinical staff, it recorded the clinical performance of the patient and the options are in numerical ranges. The ranges are at most 10% apart which can discriminate the performances among the patients.

References:

Juniper, E. F., O’byrne, P. M., Guyatt, G. H., Ferrie, P. J. & King, D. R. (1999) Development and validation of a questionnaire to measure asthma control. European respiratory journal 14(4): 902-907.

Krosnick, J. A. (2018) Questionnaire design. In The Palgrave handbook of survey research: 439-455.

This activity asked us to evaluate one of the questionnaires on its questions and format. From this activity, I have learned to appreciate how different ways the questionnaire is set will affect the data collected. For example, if the scale does not have enough data points, the audience may not be able to map their response to the scale efficiently (Krosnick, 2018) and causing the later data analysis part less accurate.

Reference:

Krosnick, J. A. (2018) Questionnaire design. In The Palgrave handbook of survey research: 439-455.

Units 7-9 Collaborative discussion 2

Initial Post

In this case, Abi, who is a researcher, has received the job of reviewing the nutritional values of a cereal product – Whizzz. As the manufacturer of the cereal product may make use of the research result from Abi as advertising materials, and the general public may not have the professional knowledge to analyse the claim themselves, the ethics in doing research by Abi is very important.

First of all, regarding the analysis of data, as long as the analysis is carried out based on factual information and the results are interpreted accurately, analysis that lead to different conclusions is not uncommon. However, when recording the analysis results, Abi should clearly record the approach he used and list the assumptions of the approach to uphold the integrity of his research (World Conferences on Research Integrity, N.D.).

If the analyses have both positive and negative results, Abi should present both of them. Even-though the project is from the manufacturer, Abi is obligated to present accurate and unbiased results as a way to uphold institutional integrity under situations of conflict of interest (Pimple, 2002). Especially when it is known that the manufacturer would probably make use of the positive results to market the product, Abi should state the negative results also in the source report for reference. In the published report, Abi should also state clearly the conflict of interest (World Conferences on Research Integrity, N.D.).

Although Abi has no control on how the others will use his program results, if he suspects the results are being misused, he should reach out to his supervisor or the client and try to expression his concerns. If no rectify actions have been taken, Abi should consider to escalate the action to related departments or professional bodies for follow up (World Conferences on Research Integrity, N.D.). Related regulations include the Nutrition and Health Claims by the European Commission. It ensures the claims on the food cannot be misleading (European Commission, N.D.)
